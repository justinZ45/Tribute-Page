Stan Lee

Creating the heroes we love today - Excelsior!

Notable achievements

    1941 - Made his comic-book debut at Timely Comics with ‘Captain America Foils the Traitor's Revenge’.
    1942 - Stan lee enlists in the United States Army, during the height of World War 2. He was chosen to work in the Army's Training Film Division.
    1961 - Lee's first ever introduction of the Fantastic Four showed that heros deal with recognizable issues. This theme became apparent in most of his work.
    1962 - Along with artist Steve Ditko, They debuted Spider-Man, which would become one of Marvel's most recognizable characters.
    1962 - Seeking to create a comic that appealed to adults, Stan Lee and Jack Kirby produce The Incredible Hulk, another hero who would later join the Avengers.
    1963 - With the success of Spider-Man and The Incredible Hulk, Lee and Kirby created a new team of heroes with inborn powers. The series was dubbed the “X-Men” and featured a mostly teenage team.
    2017 - After being awarded with a star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame, Lee was an Emmy nominee for Outstanding Short Form Animated Program.
    2008 - 2019 - Lee makes multiple appearances/cameos in the Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU) starting with Iron Man, and ending with Avengers Endgame.
